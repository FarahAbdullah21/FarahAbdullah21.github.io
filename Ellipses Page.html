Ellipses.

noun

a regular oval shape, traced by a point moving in a plane so that the sum of its distances from two other points (the foci) is constant, or resulting when a cone is cut by an oblique plane which does not intersect the base.

Equation.

x^2 / a^2 + y^2 / b^2 = 1

Real Life Examples of Ellipses.

Elliptical Trainers.

An elliptical training machines simulates the motions of running or walking. While using an Elliptical Machine the path your feet take when traced makes an elliptical shape.

Atoms.

The way that an atom is composed, the protons and neutrons are located in the center while the electrons are left orbiting outside. When tracing the path that electrons take, multiple elliptical shapes appear.

Orbit

All planets that orbit around the sun travel in an elliptical path, they all just differ in size. Kepler in 1602 said he believed that the orbit mars took was an oval, later he discovered is was an ellipse with the sun at one focus.
