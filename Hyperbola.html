Hyperbola.

noun

a symmetrical open curve formed by the intersection of a circular cone with a plane at a smaller angle with its axis than the side of the cone.

Equation.

x^2 / a^2 - y^2 / b^2 = 1

Real Life Examples of Hyperbolas.

Kobe Port Tower.

Location: Tokyo, Japan

The Kobe Tower was opened November 21, 1963 and stands at 354ft. The Kobe Tower consists of an hourglass shape, meaning that it has two hyperbolas.

Lamp Reflections.

An ordinary lamp that is pressed against a wall can also display a hyperbola.

A Sonic BOOM.

A Sonic Boom occurs when a plane beings to travel faster then the speed of sound, in 1953 a pilot flew over an Air Force Base and damaged every building when flying faster then the a speed of sound. When flying at that speed a cone shaped wave appears. The shape of this hyperbola is also known as the sonic boom curve, and where the cone meets the ground is where the hyperbola is found. The speed where this can be achieved exactly is around 717 miles per hour, a double boom can be achieved when flying at Mach 1 (767 miles per hour)
